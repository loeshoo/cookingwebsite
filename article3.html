Cooking techniques

Packed with tips and tricks to elevate your cooking, you'll find everything from sharpening your knife skills to exploring different cooking techniques and understanding food safety. There's always something new to learn, and thyme flies when you're having fun in the kitchen!

Grocery shopping can feel like a big expense, especially for students on a tight budget. Between balancing school, work, and social life, it’s easy to fall into the trap of buying convenience foods or ordering takeout. However, with some smart planning and a few practical tips, you can keep your grocery costs down and still eat nutritious, delicious meals. In this article, we’ll explore how to stretch your budget while grocery shopping without compromising on quality or taste.

Plan your meals ahead

One of the best ways to stay on budget is to plan your meals for the week ahead. This doesn’t have to be a rigid plan—just a general outline of what you’d like to eat. By doing this, you create a clear shopping list of only the items you need, which helps reduce impulse buying. Start by checking what ingredients you already have in your pantry or fridge and build your meals around them. For example, if you have pasta, you can plan a simple pasta dish one night and maybe use the leftovers for a different dish the next day. Try to overlap ingredients in different meals so nothing goes to waste. For instance, a bunch of spinach can be used in salads, sandwiches, or even blended into smoothies. A good tip is to leave one or two meal slots open for spontaneous cravings or leftovers. This way, you avoid over-purchasing, and you’ll have flexibility without needing to throw anything out.
