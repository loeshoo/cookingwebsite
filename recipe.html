Easy poke bowl with salmon

Poke bowls are the perfect way to make use of your leftover veggies.

Serves 2

45 minutes

Skill level: beginner

Hero section

Ingredients

Ingredients Toppings Sauces and Garnish
200g sushi rice 1 cucumber 30ml soy sauce
30ml rice vinegar 1 carrot, julienned 15ml sesame oil
5g sugar 50g edamame beans 5g sesame seeds for garnish
2g salt 200g fresh salmon 1 sheet of nori

The recipe

Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Cook the rice according to the package instructions. Once the rice is cooked, mix the rice vinegar, sugar and salt in a small bowl. If the sugar doesn’t dissolve quickly enough, you can put the mixture in a small pan on low heat for a couple minutes.

Gently mix the rice vinegar mixture with the rice by using a spoon or spatula. Scoop from the bottom of the bowl and scoop the rice up, turning them over slowly without stirring. This helps to distribute the mixture evenly without “breaking” the rice grains.

Cube the salmon. If you wish to eat the salmon cooked, season the salmon filet with a pinch of salt and pepper. Heat a small amount of sunflower oil or sesame oil in a pan on medium heat. Cook the salmon for about 3 to 4 minutes on each side, until it’s golden brown and cooked through.

Mix the soy sauce and sesame oil and marinate the fish in this mixture to coat it evenly. Prepare the cucumber, carrot and edamame beans.

Add the toppings on top of the rice and drizzle with soy sauce. Garnish with sesame seeds and nori if desired.

Enjoy your delicious and easy poke bowl!
